I’m an Applied Scientist at Amazon, building and optimizing large language models for real-world applications at scale. My research explores the role of knowledge in intelligent systems. I am interested in how intelligent systems acquire, integrate, and utilize information to support reasoning and decision making, and how these capabilities can be translated into practical systems operating under real-world constraints.

Currently, on the Prime Video Search team, I work on real-time query understanding for agentic search, integrating mid-sized language models into production systems that serve massive search traffic under strict latency constraints. My recent work focuses on entity-grounded query understanding, efficient inference, and connecting language-model reasoning with real-world information systems.

Previously, I was on the Stores Foundational AI team, where I developed domain-specialized language models for shopping applications and studied how new information can be integrated into foundation models while preserving their general-purpose capabilities.

Before joining Amazon, I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from the Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (KAIST) in 2022, advised by Alice Oh. During my Ph.D., I worked on question answering, retrieval, and representation learning, and interned as an Applied Scientist at Amazon and as a Research Scientist at Adobe.
